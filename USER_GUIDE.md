## Analyzing Your First Deal

### Step 1: Property Information

Navigate to **Analyze** ‚Üí **New Deal** and enter:

- **Address**: Full street address (e.g., "123 Main Street")
- **City**: City name (e.g., "Toronto")
- **Province**: Select from dropdown (ON, BC, AB, QC, NS)
- **Postal Code**: Canadian postal code (e.g., "M1M 1M1")
- **Property Type**: Single Family, Duplex, Triplex, Fourplex, Multi-Unit (5+)

### Step 2: Financial Details

#### Purchase & Financing

- **Purchase Price**: Total property cost
- **Down Payment %**: Enter percentage (5-100%)
  - üìå *Tip: <20% requires CMHC insurance*
- **Interest Rate**: Your mortgage rate (e.g., 4.5%)
- **Amortization**: Loan term in years (typically 25 or 30)

#### Closing Costs

- **Legal Fees**: Typically $1,500-$2,500
- **Inspection Cost**: Usually $400-$600
- **Other Costs**: Appraisal, title insurance, etc.

*Land Transfer Tax is calculated automatically based on your province!*

### Step 3: Revenue

- **Monthly Rent**: Total rental income per month
- **Other Income**: Laundry, parking, storage (optional)

### Step 4: Expenses

Enter your monthly expenses:

- **Property Tax**: Annual amount √∑ 12
- **Insurance**: Annual premium √∑ 12
- **Utilities**: What you pay (not tenants)
- **Maintenance**: Budget 1-1.5% of property value annually
- **Property Management**: 8-10% of rent if using a PM
- **Vacancy Rate**: 5-8% is typical in most markets

### Step 5: Investment Strategy

Choose your strategy:

- **Buy & Hold**: Long-term rental income
- **BRRRR**: Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat
  - Enter renovation costs and ARV (After Repair Value)
- **Fix & Flip**: Purchase, renovate, and sell
  - Enter renovation budget and estimated sale price

### Step 6: Review Analysis

Click **"Analyze Deal"** to generate your comprehensive report!

## Understanding the Analysis

### Deal Score (A-F Grade)

Your deal receives a score out of 100 points based on:

- **Cash Flow** (30 points): Monthly positive/negative cash flow
- **Cash-on-Cash Return** (25 points): Annual return on invested capital
- **Cap Rate** (20 points): Compared to market benchmarks
- **DSCR** (15 points): Debt Service Coverage Ratio
- **Stress Test** (10 points): OSFI B-20 compliance

#### Grade Meanings:

- **A (85-100)**: Excellent deal - pursue aggressively
- **B (70-84)**: Good deal - worth considering
- **C (55-69)**: Fair deal - analyze carefully
- **D (40-54)**: Below average - proceed with caution
- **F (0-39)**: Poor deal - pass unless special circumstances

### Key Metrics Explained

#### Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)
```
Cap Rate = Net Operating Income √∑ Purchase Price √ó 100
```
- Measures property's return independent of financing
- Compare against local market averages
- Higher = better (generally 4-7% in Canadian markets)

#### Cash-on-Cash Return
```
CoC Return = Annual Cash Flow √∑ Total Cash Invested √ó 100
```
- Measures return on your actual money invested
- Target: 8-12% for good deals
- Considers leverage (mortgage) effects

#### DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
```
DSCR = Net Operating Income √∑ Annual Debt Service
```
- Measures ability to cover mortgage payments
- Lenders want 1.15-1.25 minimum
- Below 1.0 = negative cash flow

#### Gross Rent Multiplier
```
GRM = Purchase Price √∑ Annual Rent
```
- Quick property valuation metric
- Lower = better value
- Compare to similar properties

### Cash Flow Breakdown

#### Net Operating Income (NOI)
- Gross rent - Operating expenses (excluding mortgage)
- Used for cap rate and property value calculations

#### Cash Flow
- NOI - Mortgage payments
- Your actual monthly/annual profit or loss

### Warnings & Red Flags

The platform automatically flags potential issues:

- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Negative Cash Flow**: Property loses money monthly
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Low DSCR**: Insufficient income to cover debt
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **High Vacancy Assumption**: >10% vacancy rate
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Fails Stress Test**: Won't qualify under OSFI rules
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Below Market Cap Rate**: Overpriced compared to area

## Canadian-Specific Features

REI OPS‚Ñ¢ is built exclusively for Canadian investors!

### Provincial Land Transfer Tax

Automatically calculated for:

- **Ontario**: Provincial + Toronto municipal LTT
- **British Columbia**: Property Transfer Tax
- **Alberta**: Minimal transfer fees
- **Quebec**: Welcome Tax (Taxe de bienvenue)
- **Nova Scotia**: Deed Transfer Tax

**First-Time Buyer Rebates** are automatically applied where applicable!

### CMHC Insurance

When down payment <20%:

- Calculates exact CMHC premium based on down payment %
- Premium rates (2024):
  - 5-9.99%: 4.00%
  - 10-14.99%: 3.10%
  - 15-19.99%: 2.80%
- Premium added to mortgage (eligible for financing)

### OSFI B-20 Stress Test

Required for all insured mortgages:

- Must qualify at higher of:
  - Contract rate + 2%
  - 5.25% floor rate
- Platform calculates both scenarios
- Shows if deal passes/fails stress test

### Market Benchmarks

Compare your deal to 17+ Canadian cities:

- Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal, Calgary, Edmonton
- Ottawa, Winnipeg, Hamilton, Quebec City
- And more!

Benchmarks include:

- Average cap rates by property type
- Rent-to-price ratios
- Days on market
- Price appreciation trends

## Tips & Best Practices

### Getting Accurate Results

1. **Be Conservative**: Overestimate expenses, underestimate rent
2. **Real Numbers**: Use actual quotes for insurance, property tax
3. **Market Rent**: Research comparable rentals in the area
4. **Vacancy Rate**: Use local data (5-8% is typical)
5. **Maintenance**: Budget 1-1.5% of property value annually

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå **Forgetting Expenses**
- Utilities if you pay them
- Lawn care, snow removal
- Common area maintenance (condos)
- Reserve fund contributions

‚ùå **Underestimating Maintenance**
- HVAC repairs
- Roof replacement
- Plumbing, electrical
- Appliance replacement

‚ùå **Ignoring Vacancy**
- Tenant turnover
- Maintenance periods
- Market slowdowns

‚ùå **Overlooking Closing Costs**
- Land transfer tax (biggest!)
- Legal fees
- Home inspection
- Title insurance
- Appraisal fees

### Deal Analysis Workflow

1. **Quick Screen**: Run initial analysis
2. **Refine Inputs**: Get actual quotes and numbers
3. **Sensitivity Analysis**: Test different scenarios
4. **Compare**: Stack against other opportunities
5. **Due Diligence**: Property inspection, market research
6. **Final Analysis**: Make offer decision

### Using Different Strategies

#### Buy & Hold
- Focus on cash flow and cap rate
- Target: Positive cash flow + market appreciation
- Best for: Long-term wealth building

#### BRRRR
- Ensure ARV is realistic (use appraisals)
- Budget 1.5x your renovation estimate
- Calculate refinance based on 75-80% ARV
- Goal: Recover most/all initial capital

#### Fix & Flip
- Conservative sale price estimates
- Include holding costs (mortgage, tax during reno)
- Factor in realtor fees (5%) and closing costs
- Timeline: Longer = more expensive
